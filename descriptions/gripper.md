# Titan Rover: Gripper (End Effector)

<img width="398" height="321" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f867ff11-d484-4024-8659-4ae6852e8165" />

I designed the gripper—the final component of the Titan Rover arm—to generate significant force at its tips. The primary requirement was to reliably lift a 10lb payload from the floor.

<img width="423" height="486" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/06300dca-b61f-4eb4-9003-a0bfe1bc492d" /><img width="292" height="468" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d200665c-807d-4a60-9402-438734fb1fd2" />

My design incorporates a custom worm drive, enabling each end of the gripper to exert a maximum force of 42lb. By utilizing a Nema 11 motor with a 5:1 planetary gearbox and a 35:1 gear reduction from the worm drive, I achieved a remarkably robust gripping mechanism.

<img width="494" height="352" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d60025fe-662a-499a-b42d-652ffd43a5ea" />

Material selection played a crucial role in the gripper's construction. I opted for Formlabs Rigid 4000 resin to fabricate both the gripper links and the main housing, ensuring high strength and durability. Moreover, to minimize friction and maintenance, nylon gears were combined with a brass worm, thereby eliminating the need for wet lubrication.
